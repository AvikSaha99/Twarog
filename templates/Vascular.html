Vascular Lesions

Vascular lesions are abnormalities of the blood vessels, which can manifest on the skin or internal organs. They encompass a range of conditions, from benign birthmarks to potentially serious malformations. Understanding the different types, causes, and available treatments for vascular lesions is crucial for proper diagnosis and management.

Vascular lesions are composed of blood vessels that have formed incorrectly or abnormally. These lesions can appear at birth or develop later in life and vary greatly in appearance, size, and severity. While most vascular lesions are harmless and pose primarily cosmetic concerns, some may require medical intervention due to associated complications or underlying conditions.

Diagnosis of vascular lesions typically involves a physical examination by a dermatologist or specialist. Imaging studies such as ultrasound, MRI, or CT scans may be used to assess the extent and nature of deeper or more complex lesions. In some cases, a biopsy may be performed to confirm the diagnosis and rule out other conditions.
